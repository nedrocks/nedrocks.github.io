Early Agile OR The Agile That Doesn't Work For Your Team is the Same Agile That Doesn't Work For Others

by , at 07 December 2019, category : Management

While researching the history of the standup meeting, I came across Jim Coplien's 1994 retrospective on development of Borland Quattro Pro for Windows. In it, Jim analyzes a herculean effort where a team of eight developers wrote over 1 million lines of code over a 31 month period, or approximately 1,000 lines of code per week per developer1. During development the team stumbled across early core agile principles almost a decade before the Agile Manifesto would be published.
